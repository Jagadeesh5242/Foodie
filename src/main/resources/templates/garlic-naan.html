Soft Indian flatbread with garlic and herbs

About Garlic Naan

Garlic Naan is a popular Indian flatbread brushed with garlic, butter, and herbs. It's soft, fluffy, and the perfect accompaniment to curries. Traditionally cooked in a tandoor, it can also be made in a home oven or skillet.

Ingredients (Dough)

  • 3 cups All-Purpose Flour
  • 1/2 cup Yogurt
  • 1/4 cup Milk
  • 1 tablespoon Sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon Salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon Baking Powder
  • 2 tablespoons Oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon Baking Soda
  • Warm water as needed

Ingredients (Topping)

  • 4 tablespoons Butter
  • 6-8 Garlic Cloves (minced)
  • Fresh Coriander (chopped)
  • Fresh Parsley (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, salt, sugar, baking powder, and baking soda in a bowl.
  2. Add yogurt and oil. Mix well.
  3. Add milk and warm water gradually to form soft dough.
  4. Knead for 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Apply oil and cover. Let it rest for 4-6 hours.
  6. Divide dough into 8 balls. Roll each into a 1/4-inch thick circle.
  7. Heat a skillet or tawa on high heat until very hot.
  8. Place naan on the hot skillet and cook for 1-2 minutes until bubbles form.
  9. Flip and cook the other side until golden brown spots appear.
  10. Melt butter and mix with minced garlic and salt.
  11. Brush the warm naan with garlic butter.
  12. Sprinkle fresh coriander and serve immediately.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve hot with butter
  • Pair with any curry
  • Enjoy with dal and pickles
