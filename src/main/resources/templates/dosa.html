Crispy South Indian crepe with savory potato filling

About Dosa

Dosa is a traditional South Indian breakfast item. It's a thin, crispy crepe made from rice and lentil batter, filled with spiced potato mixture. Served with sambar and chutney, it's a complete meal.

Ingredients (Dosa Batter)

  • 1 cup Rice (soaked)
  • 1/2 cup Urad Dal (black lentils, soaked)
  • 1/4 teaspoon Fenugreek Seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Ingredients (Potato Filling)

  • 3 Potatoes (boiled and cubed)
  • 2 Onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 Green Chillies (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon Mustard Seeds
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin Seeds
  • 10-12 Curry Leaves
  • 2 tablespoons Oil
  • Turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Soak rice and urad dal separately for 4 hours.
  2. Grind them together with fenugreek seeds and salt until smooth.
  3. Let batter ferment for 8-12 hours.
  4. For filling: Heat oil, add mustard and cumin seeds.
  5. Add curry leaves, onions, and green chillies.
  6. Add boiled potatoes, turmeric, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Heat a non-stick pan (dosa tava) or griddle.
  8. Pour batter and spread into a thin circle.
  9. Pour oil around edges and cook until crispy.
  10. Add potato filling on one half.
  11. Fold dosa in half and serve hot.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve with Sambar (lentil soup)
  • Pair with Coconut Chutney
  • Add Mint Chutney on the side
