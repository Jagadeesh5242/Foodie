Soya Biryani

Soya Biryani is a delicious and protein-rich vegetarian alternative to traditional meat biryani. Made with soya chunks (also known as meal maker), fragrant basmati rice, aromatic spices, and fresh herbs, this dish delivers a satisfying and flavorful meal. Soya chunks absorb spices beautifully and develop a juicy texture similar to meat, making them a popular choice for vegetarian biryani lovers. When cooked with caramelized onions, yogurt, mint, and coriander using the dum technique, the biryani becomes incredibly aromatic and rich in flavor.

🧂 Ingredients (Serves 4)

🍚 2 cups Basmati Rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
🥩 1 cup Soya Chunks
🧅 2 Onions (thinly sliced)
🍅 2 Tomatoes (chopped)
🥛 ½ cup Yogurt
🌿 Mint Leaves
🌿 Coriander Leaves
🌶 Biryani Masala
🧄 Ginger Garlic Paste
🧂 Salt & Whole Spices

👨‍🍳 Cooking Process

1 Boil soya chunks in salted water for about 5 minutes. Drain and squeeze out excess water to remove the raw smell.
2 Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Cook the rice until it is about 70% done and set it aside.
3 Heat oil or ghee in a pot and add whole spices like cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and bay leaf.
4 Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown. Keep some fried onions aside for garnishing.
5 Add ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, yogurt, and biryani masala. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
6 Add the boiled soya chunks and cook for 5–6 minutes so they absorb the spices.
7 Layer partially cooked rice over the soya masala. Sprinkle mint leaves, coriander leaves, and fried onions.
8 Cover the pot and cook on low heat (dum) for about 15–20 minutes until the flavors blend perfectly.
