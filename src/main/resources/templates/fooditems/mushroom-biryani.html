Mushroom Biryani

Mushroom Biryani is a flavorful vegetarian variation of the classic Indian biryani. Tender mushrooms are cooked with fragrant basmati rice, aromatic spices, herbs, and yogurt to create a rich and comforting dish. Mushrooms absorb spices beautifully, giving the biryani a deep umami flavor and a juicy texture. When cooked using the traditional dum method, the rice and mushrooms blend perfectly with caramelized onions, mint, and coriander, resulting in a delicious one-pot meal loved across India.

🧂 Ingredients (Serves 4)

🍚 2 cups Basmati Rice (soaked 30 minutes)
🍄 250g Fresh Mushrooms (sliced)
🧅 2 Onions (thinly sliced)
🍅 2 Tomatoes (chopped)
🥛 ½ cup Yogurt
🌿 Mint Leaves
🌿 Coriander Leaves
🌶 Biryani Masala
🧄 Ginger Garlic Paste
🧂 Salt & Whole Spices

👨‍🍳 Cooking Process

1 Wash and soak basmati rice for about 30 minutes. Cook the rice until it is 70% done and set it aside.
2 Heat oil or ghee in a large pot. Add whole spices such as cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and bay leaf.
3 Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown. Keep some fried onions aside for garnishing.
4 Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes become soft.
5 Add sliced mushrooms, yogurt, and biryani masala. Cook for a few minutes until the mushrooms release their juices.
6 Layer the partially cooked rice over the mushroom masala. Sprinkle mint, coriander, and fried onions.
7 Cover the pot tightly and cook on low heat (dum) for about 15–20 minutes until the flavors blend perfectly.
