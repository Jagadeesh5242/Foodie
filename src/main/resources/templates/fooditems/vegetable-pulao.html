Hyderabadi Veg Biryani/Pulao

Hyderabadi Veg Biryani is one of India's most celebrated rice dishes, known for its rich aroma and layered cooking technique called **dum**. Originating from the royal kitchens of Hyderabad, this vegetarian version combines fragrant basmati rice, marinated vegetables, yogurt, and traditional spices. The slow dum cooking allows every grain of rice to absorb the flavor of spices, herbs, and caramelized onions, creating a biryani that is both flavorful and aromatic.

🧂 Ingredients (Serves 4)

🍚 2 cups Basmati Rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
🥔 1 Potato (cubed)
🥕 1 Carrot (chopped)
🫘 ½ cup Green Beans
🥦 ½ cup Cauliflower florets
🧅 2 Onions (thinly sliced)
🍅 2 Tomatoes (chopped)
🥛 ½ cup Yogurt
🌿 Mint and Coriander Leaves
🌶 Hyderabadi Biryani Masala

👨‍🍳 Cooking Process

1 Wash and soak the basmati rice for about 30 minutes. This helps the rice cook evenly and stay fluffy.
2 Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottom pot and sauté whole spices like cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and bay leaf.
3 Add sliced onions and cook until golden brown. Remove some fried onions and keep them aside for garnishing.
4 Add ginger-garlic paste and tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil begins to separate.
5 Add chopped vegetables, yogurt, and biryani masala. Mix well and cook for 5–6 minutes.
6 Layer partially cooked rice over the vegetable masala. Sprinkle mint leaves, coriander leaves, and fried onions.
7 Cover the pot tightly and cook on low heat (dum) for about 20 minutes so the flavors blend beautifully.
