Paneer Biryani

Paneer Biryani is a rich and aromatic vegetarian variation of the classic Indian biryani. Instead of meat, soft cubes of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) are cooked with fragrant basmati rice, yogurt, herbs, and traditional spices. This dish combines the creamy texture of paneer with the deep flavors of caramelized onions, mint, coriander, and biryani masala. When cooked using the dum technique, the paneer absorbs the aromatic spices and the rice becomes perfectly flavorful, making Paneer Biryani a satisfying and comforting meal.

🧂 Ingredients (Serves 4)

🍚 2 cups Basmati Rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
🧀 250g Paneer Cubes
🧅 2 Onions (thinly sliced)
🍅 2 Tomatoes (chopped)
🥛 ½ cup Yogurt
🌿 Mint Leaves
🌿 Coriander Leaves
🌶 Biryani Masala
🧄 Ginger Garlic Paste
🧂 Salt & Whole Spices

👨‍🍳 Cooking Process

1 Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Cook the rice until it is about 70% done and keep it aside.
2 Heat oil or ghee in a large pot and add whole spices such as cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and bay leaf.
3 Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown. Remove some fried onions for garnishing later.
4 Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil separates.
5 Add yogurt, biryani masala, and paneer cubes. Cook gently for a few minutes so the paneer absorbs the spices.
6 Layer the partially cooked rice over the paneer masala. Sprinkle mint leaves, coriander leaves, and fried onions.
7 Cover the pot tightly and cook on low heat (dum) for about 15–20 minutes so the flavors blend together.
