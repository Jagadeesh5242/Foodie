Fragrant basmati rice with aromatic spices

About Biryani Rice

Biryani Rice is the perfect complement to any biryani curry. It's aromatic basmati rice infused with whole spices, creating a fragrant and flavorful rice dish that's essential for an authentic biryani experience.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Basmati Rice (soaked)
  • 4 cups Water
  • 4 tablespoons Ghee or Oil
  • 1 large Onion (sliced)
  • 4 Cloves
  • 2 Bay Leaves
  • 1 inch Cinnamon Stick
  • 2 Green Cardamom Pods
  • 1 Black Cardamom Pod
  • 1 Star Anise
  • 1 teaspoon Black Cumin Seeds
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat ghee in a large pot and fry sliced onions until golden. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same ghee, add whole spices (cloves, bay leaves, cinnamon, cardamom, star anise, cumin seeds).
  4. Let the spices splutter for 30 seconds.
  5. Add 4 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  6. Add salt to the water.
  7. Drain the soaked rice and add to boiling water.
  8. Cook on high heat until rice is 70% cooked (about 6-8 minutes).
  9. Drain the rice in a colander.
  10. In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer the partially cooked rice with fried onions.
  11. Drizzle remaining ghee on top and cover with aluminum foil.
  12. Place the lid on top and cook on high heat for 2-3 minutes.
  13. Reduce heat to low and cook for 20-25 minutes (dum cooking).
  14. Turn off heat and let it rest for 5 minutes before opening.
  15. Gently mix with a fork to separate the grains.
  16. Serve hot as a side to biryani curry.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve as side to Biryani Curry
  • Pair with Raita (yogurt condiment)
  • Accompany with Salad
