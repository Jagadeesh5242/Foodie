    Biryani Rice

    Fragrant basmati rice cooked with aromatic spices

    About

    Biryani Rice is the foundation of this iconic dish. Premium basmati rice layered with aromatic spices and cooked in the dum pukht method creates a fragrant, fluffy masterpiece that's a meal in itself.

    Ingredients

    • 3 cups Basmati Rice, soaked for 30 minutes
    • 4 cups Water
    • 4 tablespoons Ghee
    • 2 onions, sliced
    • 3 green cardamom pods
    • 2 black cardamom pods
    • 3 cloves
    • 1 inch Cinnamon Stick
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 2 tablespoons Ginger-Garlic Paste
    • 1/4 cup Fresh Mint and Coriander, chopped
    • 2 Green Chilies, sliced
    • Salt to taste

    Instructions

    1. Boil water with whole spices and salt.
    2. Add soaked and drained rice to boiling water.
    3. Cook until 70% done, then drain immediately.
    4. Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot.
    5. Brown onions until golden and crispy.
    6. Remove half the onions and set aside.
    7. Layer partially cooked rice over remaining onions.
    8. Sprinkle ginger-garlic paste, mint, coriander, and green chilies.
    9. Top with reserved fried onions and remaining ghee.
    10. Cover with foil and place tight-fitting lid.
    11. Cook on high heat for 2-3 minutes until steam forms.
    12. Reduce heat to low and cook for 45 minutes (dum cooking).
    13. Turn off heat and let rest for 5 minutes without opening.
    14. Gently open and fluff with a fork.
    15. Serve hot, garnishing with fresh herbs.
