A fragrant and delicious Indian rice dish

About Biryani

Biryani is one of the most beloved Indian dishes, known for its rich flavors and aromatic spices. It's a one-pot meal that combines basmati rice with meat (chicken, mutton, or fish) and a blend of traditional Indian spices. This dish is perfect for special occasions and celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Basmati Rice
  • 500g Chicken/Mutton (cut into pieces)
  • 2 Large Onions (sliced)
  • 1 cup Yogurt
  • 4 Green Chillies (chopped)
  • 1 Tablespoon Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/4 cup Mint Leaves (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup Cilantro (chopped)
  • 4-5 Cloves
  • 2 Bay Leaves
  • 1 Inch Cinnamon Stick
  • 1 Star Anise
  • 1 Teaspoon Black Cumin Seeds
  • 4 Green Cardamom Pods
  • 2 Black Cardamom Pods
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Saffron Strands
  • 1/2 cup Ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for cooking rice

Instructions

  1. Soak the basmati rice in water for 30 minutes, then wash thoroughly.
  2. Heat 1/4 cup ghee in a pan and fry half of the sliced onions until golden brown. Set aside.
  3. In a bowl, mix yogurt with ginger-garlic paste, green chillies, mint, cilantro, and salt. Add the meat to this mixture and marinate for 30 minutes.
  4. Boil water in a large pot with salt, cloves, bay leaves, cinnamon, star anise, and black cumin seeds.
  5. Add soaked rice to boiling water and cook until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  6. In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer the partially cooked rice and marinated meat alternately, starting with meat.
  7. Dissolve saffron in 1/2 cup warm milk and pour over the rice.
  8. Top with fried onions and remaining ghee.
  9. Cover the pot with aluminum foil, then place a lid on top for dum cooking.
  10. Cook on high heat for 2-3 minutes until steam forms, then reduce to low heat.
  11. Cook for 45-50 minutes on low heat without opening the lid.
  12. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 5 minutes before opening.
  13. Gently mix the biryani with a fork and garnish with fried onions and mint.

Chef's Tips

  • Rice Quality: Always use good quality Basmati rice for authentic biryani.
  • Marination: The longer you marinate the meat, the better the flavor.
  • Dum Cooking: Do not open the lid during cooking; this steams the dish perfectly.
  • Ghee Ratio: Generous use of ghee adds richness to the dish.
  • Spice Balance: Adjust spices according to your preference.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve with Raita (yogurt-based side dish)
  • Pair with Shorba (light broth)
  • Add Pickle on the side for tangy flavor
  • Serve with fresh Salad
  • Pair with Naan or Roti for those who want extra bread
